Comrade, 

We could go on for many an hour about what is behind this course. The quick and dirty is this: this month, we will study the histories of library education during the Cold War towards a clear political analysis of the profession. This history, showing librarianship as part of the U.S. imperial apparatus, is essential to understand what is happening today, in a present with deep material and psychological repercussions from the carceral, capitalistic, imperialist state’s warfare. 

This is an invitation to you to join us in moving towards bettering our present and future by naming who our enemy is and have specific, precise actions moving forward. We have designed this course to be a foundation in both history and these terms to bring us together in study moving forward. This means we welcome disagreements and uncertainty against our arguments, with the intention that we understand each other's terms.  This course is grounded in a class analysis, particularly through the Marxist framework of historical materialism. Though words in this course have become blurred throughout time from the echoes of academia, we will ground ourselves in the first session with what this means to us. All primary sources are in this google drive folder. 

No prior experience or understanding of this needed, and each week only asks to read a short primary source. We ask you to actively work towards asking questions and being curious, to be present and participatory. We welcome discussion outside of the class on our platform.
